Abstract

This project is a new version of the story "Little Red Riding Hood". In this story, we explore the emotions and strength of Red as she faces her fears. It is a simple yet meaningful story for young readers.

Index

The index gives you the exact page numbers for each topic listed in the contents.

Aim of the Project

The aim is to present a meaningful story that helps students learn values like bravery, intelligence, and kindness.

Introduction

"Little Red Riding Hood" is a fairy tale known around the world. In this version, we give a new twist that makes the story more thoughtful and educational.

Materials Used

  • Sketch pens
  • Color pencils
  • Images
  • Scissors, glue
  • A4 sheets
  • Chart paper borders

Story - Part 1

Once upon a time, there was a little girl named Red. She always wore a red hood. One day, her mother asked her to take some food to her sick grandmother who lived across the forest.

Story - Part 2

Red started walking through the forest. She was happy and singing. But suddenly, a big bad wolf appeared. He asked, "Where are you going, little girl?"

Story - Part 3

Red told the wolf she was going to visit her grandma. The wolf ran ahead and reached grandma's house before Red. He locked Grandma in a room and wore her clothes.

Story - Part 4

Red came inside and noticed something strange. "Grandma, what big eyes you have!" she said. Suddenly, the wolf jumped. But Red was smart. She called the woodcutter nearby who saved Grandma and Red.

Symbolism Analysis

The red hood shows bravery. The forest shows danger. The wolf is the fear we must fight. The woodcutter is help that comes when we are smart and brave.

Psychological Meaning

This story tells us to face our fears. It teaches that even young children can be brave and clever.

Character Study: Red

Red is smart, kind, and brave. She listens to her mother, cares for her grandma, and saves herself in danger.

Character Study: Wolf

The wolf is clever but mean. He tries to trick Red but fails in the end.

Literary Tools Used

  • Dialogue
  • Metaphor (Red Hood as protection)
  • Suspense
  • Happy Ending

Observations

I observed how a simple story can teach big lessons. Red is a great example for kids like us.

Learnings

I learnt to never trust strangers, to stay strong, and to think quickly in tough situations.

Conclusion

This project helped me learn about storytelling and courage. I enjoyed reimagining Red's story in a fun and creative way.
