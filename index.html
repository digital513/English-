Aim of the Project

To present a creatively psychological twist to a traditional fairy tale and develop storytelling with emotional depth and illustrations.

Story: The Red Hooded Girl

Red wasn’t just a girl in a cloak. She was a shadow of trauma. Her hood was a barrier to her fears. The forest was her mind. One evening she walked deeper, where memories whispered. The Wolf wasn’t real — he was her inner voice, a symbol of fear.

When she reached her grandmother’s house, it was empty. But she wasn’t afraid. The Wolf emerged from the shadows, but Red didn’t run. She spoke, "I know you now. You're my fear, and I’m not scared anymore." She removed her hood and smiled — finally free.

This was no fairytale ending. It was a new beginning.

Observation and Analysis

  • Red = trauma
  • Forest = confused mind
  • Wolf = hidden fear
  • Grandma's house = healing space

Conclusion

The tale reflects emotional strength. Through symbolism, children can be taught about feelings, courage, and facing one’s fears.
