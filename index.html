Aim of the Project

The aim is to retell a classic fairy tale through a modern, psychological perspective. It helps in understanding inner fears and encourages creative writing and analysis.

Introduction

The story of Little Red Riding Hood is well known. But what if Red was more than a girl—what if she symbolized emotional trauma? This project explores that interpretation through story.

Procedure (Story)

Red wore her hood to hide her past. Her path through the forest was a walk through her own thoughts. The Wolf wasn’t just a danger—it was her fear. At her grandmother’s house, she faced it and overcame it.

Observation and Analysis

Each part of the story has meaning. The forest is Red’s mind. The Wolf is her fear. The house is her place of healing. Removing her hood shows she is finally free.

Inferences

  • Stories can reflect emotions.
  • Symbolism makes stories deeper.
  • Facing fear leads to strength.
  • Creative writing is a powerful tool.

Conclusion

This story shows how a well-known tale can be told with a new meaning. It helped in building creativity and understanding emotions in a unique way.
