This is to certify that [Your Name] of Class VII has successfully completed this English project titled "A Happier Ending to Little Red Riding Hood" under the guidance of [Teacher's Name].

Abstract

This project reimagines the classic tale of Little Red Riding Hood with a positive twist. It explores themes of kindness, empathy, and creative thinking while promoting a happy resolution.

Introduction

The original story of Little Red Riding Hood is a cautionary tale about danger and trust. In today’s world, we wish to present a version that highlights understanding and peace.

Aim of the Project

To recreate a happier version of the ending of Little Red Riding Hood and understand how creative storytelling can influence morals and feelings.

Materials Used

  • Storybook
  • Notebook and pen
  • Color pencils and markers
  • Printed pictures and glue

Procedure

I read the original story, imagined a happier version, wrote a new ending, created illustrations, and compiled all elements into a creative project.

Original Ending Summary

The wolf eats Grandma and tries to eat Red Riding Hood. A woodcutter arrives and rescues them. The wolf is punished, ending the story with fear and danger.

My Happier Ending

Red offers the wolf cookies instead of running away. The wolf, touched by her kindness, promises to be good. They all have tea together and become friends.

Observation and Analysis

Changing the ending made the story more uplifting. It encourages children to believe in second chances, peace, and communication.

Inference

This project helped me understand how a storyteller can shape emotions through plot choices. Stories are powerful and can inspire positive values.

Conclusion

Creative writing is not just fun, but it’s also powerful. I learned to imagine, write, draw, and express my ideas through this project.
